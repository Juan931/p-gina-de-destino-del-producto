The Beatles

The Beatles were an English rock band2 active during the 1960s, and recognized as the most commercially successful and most critically acclaimed in the history of popular music and rock music. 3 4 5 6 7 Formed in Liverpool, it was formed from 1962 by John Lennon (rhythm guitar, vocalist), Paul McCartney (bass, vocalist), George Harrison (lead guitar, vocalist) and Ringo Starr (drums, vocalist). Rooted in the skiffle, beat music and rock and roll of the 1950s, his sound would often incorporate elements of classical music and traditional pop, among others, in innovative ways in his songs; the band would later come to work with a wide range of musical styles, ranging from ballads and Indian music, to psychedelia and even hard rock. The nature of his enormous popularity, which had first emerged with the "beatlemania craze, was transformed as his compositions became more sophisticated. They came to be perceived as the embodiment of progressive ideals, extending their influence in the social and cultural revolutions of the 1960s.
